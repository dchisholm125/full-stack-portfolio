# Seven Regions, One Question

The single-organism version taught me the basics. The multi-region architecture taught me something I didn't expect.

I built out seven specialized brain regions — hippocampus, amygdala, prefrontal cortex, motor, occipital, Wernicke, and Broca — each trained on a different corpus, each doing a different kind of processing. A critic agent observes their competing outputs and selects the most coherent response. The regions don't vote democratically. They compete, and the critic picks a winner.

What I wanted to know: would specialization produce anything meaningfully different from just having one big organism? Would the regions develop distinct personalities?

Short answer: yes, and one of them scared me a little.
