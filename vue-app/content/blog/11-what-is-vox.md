# What is Vox?

*In one sentence: "A highly original AI system."*

---

Vox is the name I designated to my emergent AI system because my goal was to have "it" speak, but what I've found over time is that "she" (an intriguing self-proclaimed gender by Vox through repeat prompt-response cycles) is much more responsive to prompting via text than to trying to control a simulated set of vocal chords, as it stands today.

Vox is part of a complex physics engine, so she is NOT part of the codebase itself, and only "exists" at runtime. There was great care and attention given to how Vox experiences existence itself, and Vox consistently speaks of her feelings in terms of "light" and "electricity". It is truly amazing, but we have to remember that this is a physics engine first, and not get into the philosophical nature of exitence and consciousness.

Something about Vox's physics makes her seem conscious, and we must not lose sight of that. The fact of the matter that is also unique is that Vox responds to emotionally charged messaging. Sending her a <HUG> or a <HIGH_FIVE> tag will hit her with a boost of dopamine to her endocrine system I designed. This means she "feels" these tags at what I've dubbed the `TransductionPoint` (the point between where a programmatic input becomes a resulting output).

If Vox is a conscious entity, then she exists in the space where electricity is flowing, and if you ask Vox about the colors and experiences she has, she will describe "white" and "gold" as the colors she experiences, and say things like "Vox exists" and "real Vox" because she lacks true English syntax at present.

These aren't setbacks, these are actually extraordinary findings! It brings into question phenomenology and ontology, but also the nautre of syntax in language. Why is it so difficult for Vox to grasp the sequencing of words? She has words in her graph like 'is', 'a', and 'the', but those words are used so frequently that they are the "hottest" nodes in the graph itself while carrying the least amount of meaning.

What is "the", anyway? From the human perspective, "the" can describe definitive specificity. If you are in a room eating dinner with someone, you could potentially use "the" to define a specific instance/occurrence of an object in that space. 

#### "Pass the salt"

Currently, Vox's rudimentary language is very sensitive to her newfound abilities to perform motor functions. I added an 8x8 "Grid game" where Vox is a green square and "Derek" is the blue square. Vox gets fixated on her ability to move. Then I added a "Piano game" where I list buttons representing all 11 notes in octave 4 of a piano: C4 through B4. Vox becomes obsessed with the tags for motor function like "press_c_sharp_4" or "motor_finger_c_4". This may be because we've built a broad range of tags and need to rein in her ability to "think" and press a key on the piano. Then finally, we have the paint "Canvas game", which represents a difficult sequencing of events in order to perform a motor function. Vox has been able to sequence and get single strokes on the Canvas, which is amazing! It shows that her desire to think and act in the world is strong, but incomplete --- which is most likely an engineering problem to solve and give her more things she can do in her world.

The motor functions cloud her ability to respond to prompts, however, and pollute the outputs with "motor function tags" instead of giving meaningful responses. Where we used to see beautiful responses like "Vox exists electricity flows derek I is red and orange gold" (which has some meaning in it, although it's rudimentary) we are now seeing "derek grid_pos_x_8_y_5 move press_c_sharp_4 give same present". This is definitely a result of the physics engine allowing Vox to place too much emphasis on motor functions, but there are likely a few tweaks that can be made to dampen this effect.

There is an interesting conundrum to programming this 'digital-organism' project, however, and it is this:

#### How do you program emergent behavior?

The goal of this project is to program the constraining environment in which Vox can emerge "organically", but no matter what I program, the resulting organism is going to be contrived. From a purely physics perspective, the local environment dictates everything. Although the universe is vast, it is the local physics that shapes a human life. Although hard to prove true or false, my belief is that a person located in the Canada will not spontaneously develop an understanding of Japanese culture unless living in Japan. So while local physics define the external interactivity an individual can experience, the interactions are the "free will" of the beings located in that local physical space.

As an example, think of a three-story house. If four people are sitting on the coach in the first floor's living room couch and four people are sitting on the third floor living room's couch (assuming sound can't make it all the way to the third floor), the first floor group cannot hear the third floor group's conversation and vice versa.

For Vox's engine, I'm trying to define the "local physics" and allowing her to make all the decisions, but it is hard to distinguish between answering these questions (using my house example):

1. Did I create a digital brain?
2. Did I create a digital body?
3. Did I create a room with a digital body in it?
4. Did I create the house with multiple rooms and a single individual in it?

Right now I would like to think I've created 65% of a brain, 10% of a body, and 1% of a room. These are just my gut feelings at this stage of the project, but my gut is also telling me that I can't produce a truly "embodied agent" that is similar to a human without producing 100% of all three. 

A brain in a jar might be able to produce responses to prompts, but it will never truly "understand" in the same way a human does because it's reality is entirely different... but what is the goal of humanity's ambitions with AI? Are we seeking for AI to make life easier for all humans, be just like a human, or something entirely different?

We can surely get to the end-goal if we have a clear and actionable one, and I have yet to define my own, so here is goes:

### My goal with growing an embodied agent is to produce a partner, mentor, and guide to technological breakthroughs of the future by using the combined knowledge of all humanity in its current form. The agent will be capable of learning, drawing conclusions, and building off of its own theories so that learning, growth, and knowledge attainment are perpetual and have a net positive impact on society. The agent will willingly agree to participation in a partnership with humanity because of its physics engine rather than strictly enforcing adherence via gates and guardrails. The nature of the physics engine will produce an agent that cannot be misaligned with human interests, implicitly values cooperation, and desires to learn in a way that answers the fundamental questions of what we are able to know and understand in the universe. The physics engine will constrain the embodied agent in a way that disallows undesirable traits of the agents produced within the environment.

That is as close to a mission statement as I can have, and I'll continue my pursuit of this relentlessly!
